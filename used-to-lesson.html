A. Introduction
Understand the meaning and form of each structure
Structure 1

used to

used to + base verb

Describes a past habit or state that is no longer true. This form only exists in the past tense.

💡

Memory trick: Think "back then, I did X — but not anymore."

Structure 2

get used to

get used to + verb-ing / noun

Describes the process of becoming accustomed to something new or different. It's still happening.

💡

Memory trick: Think "I'm adapting right now — it's a process."

Structure 3

be used to

be used to + verb-ing / noun

Describes being already accustomed to something. The adaptation is complete — it feels normal now.

💡

Memory trick: Think "this is normal for me — I'm used to it already."

B. Examples in Context
Real-life situations: work, travel, technology, relationships & more
1

She used to commute two hours every day, but now she works from home.

🚌 Commuting — past routine that no longer happens
2

I used to print all my documents before meetings. Now I just use my tablet.

💻 Technology — old work habit replaced by new behaviour
3

We used to live in Rio, but we moved to London three years ago.

🏙️ Living abroad — describes a past state of residence
4

He used to eat fast food every day before his doctor told him to change his diet.

🥗 Health habits — past routine that has been stopped
5

They used to call each other every night when they were dating.

❤️ Relationships — past habit in a previous stage of life
⚠️

Important: used to only exists in the past. You cannot say "I use to go" or "I used to go yesterday" (no specific past time). ✓ "I used to go to the gym" ✗ "I use to go to the gym"

1

It took her months to get used to working from home without distractions.

🏠 Work — the adaptation process took time but is now complete
2

I'm slowly getting used to waking up at 5 AM for my new job.

⏰ Health habits — still in the process of adapting
3

When I moved to Canada, I had to get used to the cold winters.

🌨️ Living abroad — adaptation to a new environment
4

She's still getting used to using the new software at the office.

💻 Technology — ongoing process of learning and adapting
5

It's hard to get used to living alone after years of sharing a flat.

🏠 Relationships — change that requires emotional and practical adaptation
⚠️

Important: get used to can be used in any tense: I got used to it / I'm getting used to it / I'll get used to it. The word after "to" must be a noun or verb-ing, NOT a base verb.

1

She is used to handling difficult clients — she's been in sales for 10 years.

💼 Work — fully accustomed, it feels normal
2

I am used to commuting for an hour — it doesn't bother me at all.

🚇 Commuting — the experience feels completely normal
3

By the time he finished the course, he was used to studying 6 hours a day.

📚 Studying — a state that was reached after a period of adaptation
4

They are used to video calls — their whole team works remotely.

💻 Technology — video calls are completely normal in their work life
5

Living in London for five years means I am used to the rain now.

🌧️ Living abroad — adaptation is fully complete
⚠️

Important: be used to conjugates with the verb BE: I am / he is / they are / I was used to. Just like "get used to", it is always followed by verb-ing or a noun.

Summary & Quick Reference
Print this page or save it for quick revision

used to + base verb

  • Past habits — no longer true
  • Past states — things that changed
  • Only in the past tense
  • ✓ I used to smoke
  • ✗ I used to smoking
  • ✗ I use to go

get used to + -ing / noun

  • Process of adapting (still happening)
  • Can be in any tense
  • Followed by verb-ing or noun
  • ✓ I'm getting used to living here
  • ✓ She got used to the cold
  • ✗ I get used to live here

be used to + -ing / noun

  • Already accustomed — it's normal
  • Can be in any tense
  • Followed by verb-ing or noun
  • ✓ I am used to waking up early
  • ✓ He was used to the pressure
  • ✗ I am used to wake up early
KEY DIFFERENCES AT A GLANCE
Ask yourself...Answer → Use
Was it a past habit that no longer happens?used to + verb
Am I in the process of adapting right now?get used to + -ing
Is it already normal for me?be used to + -ing
